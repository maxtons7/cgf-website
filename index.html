  <section id="mission">

    <h2>Our Mission</h2>

    <p>

      The Causal Governance Framework is a policy and governance innovation initiative designed by Dr. Maxton R. R. Scotland to dismantle institutional barriers for marginalised populations—particularly ethnic minority millennials. Rooted in intergenerational equity, CGF integrates artificial intelligence, open-source data, and community knowledge to forge inclusive pathways across policy, healthcare, education, and civic participation.

    </p>

  </section>


  <section id="risk">

    <h2>Risk Assessment Summary</h2>

    <p class="highlight">

      The CGF has undergone a preliminary risk assessment across technical, ethical, legal, and operational domains:

    </p>

    <ul>

      <li><strong>Data Privacy Risk:</strong> All AI-supported outputs avoid use of personal identifiers and adhere to GDPR standards.</li>

      <li><strong>Algorithmic Bias:</strong> Regular audit mechanisms are in place to detect and mitigate racial or socio-economic bias in automated systems.</li>

      <li><strong>Operational Continuity:</strong> The framework is designed to be modular, with no single point of failure in delivery or governance.</li>

      <li><strong>Community Risk:</strong> Engagements are designed to empower, not extract; no data is collected without explicit, informed consent.</li>

    </ul>

  </section>


  <section id="ai-ethics">

    <h2>AI Disclaimer</h2>

    <p>

      Artificial Intelligence tools are ethically incorporated in the Causal Governance Framework to augment—not replace—human decision-making. All AI systems utilised are subjected to rigorous scrutiny to ensure:

    </p>

    <ul>

      <li>Transparency in logic, purpose, and limitation</li>

      <li>Alignment with human rights and social justice goals</li>

      <li>Non-exploitative use of datasets from vulnerable populations</li>

      <li>Inclusion of community oversight in AI-integrated pilots</li>

    </ul>

    <p class="highlight">

      The CGF initiative does not use AI to automate final policy decisions or sensitive assessments. AI is deployed solely to generate insights, surface patterns, and test causal hypotheses under human supervision.

    </p>

  </section>
